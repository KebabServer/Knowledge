Facing 4Bet OOP: Splitting X/R Ranges into Small/Big Sizes. How to do it?

Facing a 4Bet OOP is not a great node. Once we face a 4B we shift from a high-EV 3B spot to one where we just want to lose as little as possible.

Donking

Having a Donking Range Makes Sense When

  • Your cold call range has flopped multiple sets
  • Your opponent is likely check back (AK combos have missed the board)
  • Your hand class has a clear answer vs raise
    • Don't lead lots of equity hands
    • Don't lead complete trash

Splitting X/R Ranges into Small/Big Sizes

  • Even at low SPRs, OOP should have a raising range vs small (likely range) cbets
  • First, define the bottom of your value bet range - then choose your bluffs
    • Avoid hands that suffer vs shove
  • Generally you want to have an all-in size and a small size
    • All-in range should be draws that want to capitalize on their fold equity plus value hands that need protection
  • You can almost always shove an 8-out draw or better

Make sure you play your range and NOT your specific hand vs regs

  • If the flop checks around and the turn is good for your opponent, don't get too excited with a big hand and leave your checking range unprotected
    • Check shove highest equity draws
    • Slowplay your best value hands

Try to identify spots where the pool doesn't understand their hand's equity vs a range with thin value raises

  • The wider your opponent's range, the better
  • The more sizing tell you can pick up, the better
  • The more loosely they call, the better
  • The more they cbet too weak a range, the better
