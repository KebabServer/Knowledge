Lesson 11: Playing Dynamic Ranges

(Tailored to Counter your Opponent's Range)

The ability to change your betting range on any given street in order to maximally exploit an opponent's strategy (i.e., a mental node-lock).

This is the single-most EV-increasing skill in poker (aside from a good mental game).

  • Always start with a good baseline strategy which will perform well on average - have a starting point for all the streets!
  • Make a read and put players into one of three main categories:

1. Weak Reg/Weak Fish

  • Does not protect checking ranges
  • Overfolds
  • Underbluffs

Plan: Attack their weak, vulnerable ranges.

2. Station/Spewer (Reg or Fish)

  • Likes to bluffcatch
  • Probably overcalls
  • Probably overbluffs (These often go hand in hand!)

Plan: Increase value sizing; lower value threshold.

3. Good, respectable regs

  • Bluffs but doesn't spew
  • Protects ranges
  • Not where your main win-rate comes from

Plan: Stick to baseline; play solid.
