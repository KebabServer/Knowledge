Lesson 18: Attacking Capped Ranges and Imbalanced Sizings

  • The harder it is for your opponent to arrive at a node with the correct range, the better the spot is for bluffing
  • Use range analysis to remove nutted combos from an opponent's range, street by street, in order to identify nodes where their range is vulnerable
  • The more confident you are about your opponent being capped and/or the better blockers you have, the larger you can raise when attacking
  • The better your opponent is, the more important it is for you to have a credible line with the nutted part of your range
